About Me

I am an assistant professor at Peking University specializing in Second Language Acquisition. My research focuses on eye-tracking studies, multiword expressions, and the intersection of linguistics and technology.

Research

My research centers around the cognitive processes involved in second language learning, particularly through eye-tracking technology and multiword expressions. Below are some of my key research areas:

  • Eye-tracking technology in second language reading
  • Multiword expressions in applied linguistics
  • Language learning technologies and AI applications

Publications

Here are some selected publications:

  • Yi, W. & Pulido, M. (2023). Eye-Tracking and Second Language Reading: A Cognitive Approach. Second Language Research Journal.

Current Projects

Here are some of my ongoing research projects:

  • Eye-Tracking for Language Proficiency: Using eye-tracking data to predict reading proficiency in second language learners. Learn more
  • Multiword Expressions in Real-Time: Developing AI models to track and interpret multiword expressions in second language reading.
  • Cultural Perception of Emotions in Language: Investigating how different cultures perceive emotions through language using eye-tracking data.
